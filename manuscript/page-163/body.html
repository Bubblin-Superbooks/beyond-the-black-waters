Chapter XIII.

The Expedition.

When Mr. Lawrence’s short visit was ended, Io meditated over what she had heard with self-reproach and abasement.

“I have been adding to my Oscar’s troubles,” she said to herself, “instead of trying to lighten his burden. If he has indeed made a painful sacrifice to honour and duty, shall I, by my pride and sullen gloom, show him that as regards my happiness it has been made in vain?
